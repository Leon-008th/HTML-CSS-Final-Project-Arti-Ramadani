Procedural Generation

Geology

The first step of the Procedural Generation in Retra is generating the geology. Using python and the rng integration, the code generates a list of 1050 numbers. Each number corresponds with a tile index, for example 1 = "grass tile". This random generation is controlled via the user input from the GUI. In "meadow" setting, the code cannot generate more than 600 tiles that are flowers. Then after the list is generated, it get's shuffled to increase the percieved "randomness". Then every 35 numbers are put in a seperate list. At the end, we get a randomly generated a map list using 30 nested lists.

Hydrosphere

Next we have the hydrosphere. Based on the user specifications the program figures out the lake size. It could be a "small" (3x3 tiles), "medium" (4x4) or a "large" (5x5). Based on that it chooses a random index in the map by generating 2 random numbers (x, y coordinates). Then it sets that as the beggining water tile. Then based on the random generation it goes one tile down and could start the row from 1-2 tiles behind the x coordinate. It generates for how many rows is needed then adds it to a seperate special list that connects with the 2D map.

Flora

Lastly, we have the Flora. The program has to position trees, mushrooms and bushes. Based on the user specifications the number of trees could be heigher or lower. Using the random seed generation, it generates 2 random numbers (x, y coordinates). It adds that information as a list with 3 elements: ["x", "y", "Flora_type"]. For trees, it also adds their color. Which is a number between 0-1 for either green or orange. It does this with every flora element and adds it to a seperate special list. By then end we have 3 lists that all connect to the map. And using PyGame, the programs begins "drawing" every element on the screen.

Buildings

Buildings are a fundemental part of the game. From rescource managment to strategic RTS, they cover the main functions of the game. There are 6 number of structures in the game: the Town Hall, the Farm, the Lumbermill, the Citizens complex, the Baracks and the Archery Range.

Town Hall

The Town Hall is the central structure of any settlement. It acts as the administrative core, enabling population growth, unlocking new buildings, and serving as the primary resource drop-off point. Many advanced systems and upgrades depend on the Town Hall’s level.

Citizen Complex

The Citizen Complex provides housing for the population. Each complex increases the settlement’s population capacity, allowing more workers and soldiers to be supported. Expanding housing is essential for sustained growth and economic stability.

Farm

Farms generate food over time, supplying the settlement with a renewable resource required to sustain citizens and train units. Their placement is influenced by nearby terrain, and higher efficiency ensures population growth without starvation penalties.

Lumbermill

The Lumbermill processes nearby trees into usable wood. Wood is a fundamental construction resource, required for buildings, tools, and early military units. The Lumbermill’s effectiveness depends on proximity to forested areas.

Barracks

The Barracks is responsible for training melee military units. It unlocks basic infantry and enables defensive and offensive capabilities. A functional military presence is necessary to protect the settlement and expand into contested territory.

Archery Range

The Archery Range trains ranged units specialized in long-distance combat. Archers provide strategic advantages against slower or armored enemies. Proper positioning and unit management make ranged forces critical in both defense and large-scale battles.
